# Anvil Feature Spec: Awareness Stage and Long-Form Mode

Date: June 28, 2026
Status: awareness stage is P1. Long-form mode is P2, after the Curiosity engine and the multi-rung Pain and Promise builders ship.
One line: teach Anvil where the buyer sits on the awareness ladder, then give it a long-form canvas for the coldest buyers, with compliance built in from the start because this is the most dangerous format in direct response.

## Why this exists

The biggest pool in any market is the unaware, people who do not yet know they have the problem. Short direct ads do not work on them, because a direct pitch assumes the reader already feels the problem. The unaware respond to a story or a discovery that reframes their situation and walks them down the ladder, from "nothing is wrong" to "this is my problem and here is the fix." That walk is long. It is an advertorial or a video sales letter, not a six-block feed ad. That format is what lets cold spend scale without the audience drying up.

Anvil today writes short ads for warmer buyers. It has the ingredients for the cold format, the reframe and the pain and promise rungs, but not the canvas or the awareness input that decides when to use it.

## Part 1. Awareness stage as a first-class input

Add awareness stage to the campaign settings, alongside objective and voice. Five rungs:

- Unaware. Does not know they have the problem.
- Problem-aware. Feels the problem, does not know the solutions.
- Solution-aware. Knows solution types, not your offer.
- Product-aware. Knows your offer, not yet convinced.
- Most aware. Ready, needs the deal and the call to action.

It changes two things in every generation, not just long-form:

- Format and length. Colder stages need longer, story-led copy. Warmer stages need shorter, more direct copy.
- Which block leads and how direct the call to action is. Unaware leads with the Curiosity reframe and holds the offer back. Most aware leads with the offer and conditions and a direct call to action.

This is a small input to add and it makes all output smarter immediately, even before long-form exists. It is the prerequisite for long-form, since awareness is what tells Anvil to reach for the long canvas at all.

## Part 2. Long-form mode (the advertorial)

A long-form output type, separate from the short placement previews. It is the six blocks unrolled across a narrative and sequenced by awareness:

1. A reframing hook or discovery that stops an unaware reader. This is the Curiosity engine's big idea.
2. A story or context that earns attention.
3. The problem walked slowly, the Pain Chain rungs.
4. A mechanism or characterization that explains why the problem persists and what changes it.
5. The promise, the Promise Ladder rungs.
6. Proof, from the Proof engine, matched to the size of the promise.
7. Constraints dissolved with the A.W.E. builder.
8. The offer, the Conditions, and the call to action.

Use it for the unaware and problem-aware, cold traffic. It reuses the P1 depth pieces directly, the Curiosity engine, the Pain Chain and Promise Ladder builders, the Proof engine, and the Constraints dissolve. Long-form is those pieces on a longer canvas, which is why it cannot come before them.

Output is the advertorial draft plus a matching short feeder ad whose only job is to earn the click into the long-form piece.

## Compliance, built in from the start

Long-form on the unaware is the single most dangerous format in direct response. It is where the fake-news-article, doctors-hate-this, tribal-berry copy lives, because length plus a cold audience is room to make wild claims. So the compliance posture is not bolted on later, it is part of the spec.

- The P0 caps apply with full force here and are stricter. No Taboo Solution frames, no idea caricatures, the insight dial capped for trust voices.
- No fake-article or fake-discovery framing. No disguising the ad as editorial. An advertorial must be transparent that it is advertising.
- For regulated clients, long-form must be a genuine educational story, not a claim engine wearing a story. No medical, legal, financial, or income claims, sell the free next step, human-reviewed before spend.
- Long-form is the format to hold back longest for trust markets. For the heaviest compliance tier, the licensed-board niches, long-form should be off by default and require a reviewer to sign off on the format itself, not only the claims.

Governing rule: if the format only works by deceiving the reader about what they are reading, do not ship it.

## Build order placement

- Awareness stage input: P1. Small, sequences with the depth work, improves all output.
- Long-form mode: P2. Depends on the Curiosity engine, the Pain Chain and Promise Ladder builders, and the awareness input. Gated hardest for trust markets.

## Cautions

- This is a high-skill format. Most who try it butcher it. That is creative-strategist-grade work, which reinforces the operator moat, but it is not where a new operator starts.
- Do not chase it as a shiny object. It is the natural payoff of the P1 depth on a longer canvas, not a separate direction.
- A feeder ad plus an advertorial plus a landing page is a funnel. Decide where Anvil stops and the landing-page lever begins, which ties to the P2 lever checklist.

## Open questions

- Should long-form draw on real won-or-lost results once the loop is closed, so the narrative learns from outcomes, not structure alone. Dependent on close-the-loop.
- Should the feeder ad and the advertorial share one awareness-sequenced plan, so the click and the read feel like one story.

## Appendix: the five buckets, core jobs and the towards-or-away rule

This is the operating logic for the awareness input, taken from the source cheat sheet. Each stage has a core job and a choice of posture. Towards leads with the opportunity and the benefit. Away leads with the problem and the consequence. Pick one per ad based on the buyer and the market.

- **Unaware.** Core job: bring the hidden symptom or the hidden opportunity to the surface, and create dissatisfaction with the status quo. Towards, surface an opportunity they do not know exists. Away, surface a threat they do not know exists. This is the long-form and story territory.
- **Problem aware.** Core job: move them from the symptom to your root cause, and install your view of the problem. Towards, explain what creates success. Away, explain what causes their problem. Undo or accommodate false beliefs about other approaches.
- **Solution aware.** Core job: get them to accept your specific approach as the right one, and prove differentiation. Towards, show your path to the result. Away, show your fix for the problem. Account for the solutions and the past failures they already know.
- **Product aware.** Core job: make yours the obvious choice and build vendor trust. Towards, prove you are the best path to the result. Away, prove you are the best fix, the one that addresses the root cause others miss.
- **Most aware.** Core job: give a reason to act now and remove all friction. Towards, opportunity-based urgency, get in before it is gone. Away, problem-based urgency, fix it before it gets worse.

Two cross-cutting rules from the source. Lead with your strongest material. And change the minimum number of beliefs needed to make the sale, rather than arguing everything. For trust markets, the urgency in the most-aware stage must be real, not manufactured, which is the same caution the compliance gate enforces everywhere else.
